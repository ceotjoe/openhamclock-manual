The Activation Programmes

These four programmes share a common idea: an amateur radio operator travels to a designated location — a national park, a mountain summit, a nature reserve, or a historical military bunker — sets up a portable or mobile station, and makes contacts with other operators. The "hunter" side (that is where you come in, watching OpenHamClock) tries to contact as many activators as possible to earn awards and points.

ℹ️
You do not need to be an activator to use these panels

The vast majority of operators using these panels are hunters — they stay home at their fixed station and call the portable activators. You need only a normal amateur licence and a radio. OpenHamClock shows you who is active, where they are, and what frequency they are on.

The Activation Panels

OpenHamClock has individual panels for each programme (POTA, SOTA, WWFF, WWBOTA), all following the same layout. Each panel shows a live list of active activators — operators who are on the air right now at a qualifying location. The list is sorted by most recently spotted, with the freshest activity at the top.

Reading an activator row

Callsign The activator's callsign. Click to draw the great-circle path from your QTH on the map.
Reference The park/summit/reserve identifier (e.g. US-0001 for Acadia NP, W4C/CM-001 for a SOTA summit). Links to the programme's website for that location.
Name The human-readable name of the location.
Frequency The last spotted frequency in kHz. Click to tune your rig if Rig Bridge is configured.
Band / Mode Current band and mode (SSB, CW, FT8, etc.).
Age How long since the last spot for this activator. Spots older than 30 minutes are automatically removed.

Map Markers

Every active activator gets a marker on the world map, positioned at the coordinates of their park, summit, or reserve. Marker icons are distinct per programme so you can tell them apart at a glance:

🟢 Green flag POTA — Parks on the Air activator
🔴 Red triangle SOTA — summit activator
🟣 Purple leaf WWFF — flora & fauna nature reserve
🔵 Blue shield WWBOTA — bunker / fortification

Click any marker on the map to open a popup showing the activator's callsign, reference, name, frequency, and a Tune button (if Rig Bridge is connected).

Working an Activator — Step by Step

  1. Spot an activator in the panel or on the map

    The panel refreshes every 60 seconds. New spots appear at the top with a brief highlight animation. Green flags on the map tell you which parks are currently active at a glance.

  2. Click the activator row or map marker

    This opens the details popup and draws the great-circle path from your QTH to the park on the world map. The DX Location panel updates with the bearing, distance, and the activator's sunrise/sunset times (useful if they are in a very different time zone).

  3. Click the frequency or Tune button

    If Rig Bridge is connected, your radio tunes to the exact spotted frequency in the correct mode automatically. If not, note the frequency and tune manually.

  4. Call the activator

    Most activators work simplex. Listen first — activators are usually in a short pile-up. When you hear a gap, give your callsign once, clearly. Exchange a signal report and the park reference. You are done — that is a valid hunt!

  5. Log the contact

    Log to your POTA/SOTA/WWFF account to earn credits toward awards. For POTA, upload your log at pota.app. For SOTA, use SOTAwatch.

💡
FT8 activations

Many POTA activators work FT8, especially for longer paths. If the panel shows a frequency like 14.090 MHz with mode FT8, switch WSJT-X to that frequency — the activator will appear in your decode list. In FT8 you can work DX parks that would otherwise be beyond SSB range with a modest station.

Configuration

Each programme panel can be shown or hidden independently from Settings → Panels. Additional options under Settings → POTA (and equivalent for each programme):

Show on map Toggle map markers for this programme on/off without hiding the panel.
Continent filter Show only activators on your continent, or a specific continent. Useful for smaller screens.
Mode filter Show only CW, SSB, or FT8 activators.
Audio alert Play a tone when a new activator appears. Configurable per programme.
Needs list Enter park/summit references you still need for awards — those entries highlight in a different colour.
